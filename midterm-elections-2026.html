Midterm elections take place every four years, exactly halfway through a presidential term. The 2026 midterms fall at the midpoint of Donald Trump's second term — and as with most midterms, they will serve as a national referendum on the sitting president.

The stakes are significant: control of the House and Senate determines whether Trump can pass legislation in the second half of his presidency, or whether he faces a divided — or even hostile — Congress from 2027 onward.

What is a midterm election?

The U.S. Constitution requires elections for all House seats every two years. Senators serve six-year terms, so roughly one-third of Senate seats come up for election every two years. When these elections fall in the middle of a presidential term — as in 2026 — they are called "midterm elections."

Unlike presidential elections, midterms do not determine who occupies the White House. Trump's presidency continues until January 20, 2029 regardless of the 2026 results. What midterms determine is the composition of Congress — and by extension, how much legislative power the president can exercise in the back half of their term.

What happens if Democrats win the House?

If Democrats flip the House in 2026, Trump would face a divided Congress for the final two years of his term. A Democratic House could block legislation, launch investigations, and potentially initiate impeachment proceedings — though conviction in the Senate requires a two-thirds supermajority, which would require significant Republican defections.

Historically, the president's party almost always loses seats in midterm elections. Since World War II, the president's party has lost House seats in every midterm election except 1998 (Clinton) and 2002 (Bush after 9/11). This "midterm penalty" reflects voter tendency to use midterms as a check on presidential power.

The Senate map in 2026

The Senate map in 2026 is more favorable to Republicans than the House. Of the 33 seats up for election, the majority are held by Democrats — including several in states that Trump won in 2024. This gives Republicans a structural advantage in maintaining or even expanding their Senate majority, regardless of the overall national political environment.

Key number: Republicans currently hold a narrow House majority. Democrats need to flip a relatively small number of seats to take control — making the House the more competitive chamber in 2026.

Frequently asked questions

When are the 2026 midterm elections?

November 3, 2026. All federal elections in the United States are held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November.

Can Trump be removed through the midterms?

No. Midterm elections cannot remove a sitting president. Trump's term runs until January 20, 2029 regardless of the results. The only constitutional mechanisms for removing a president are impeachment (House vote + Senate conviction) or the 25th Amendment.

How do midterms affect the 2028 presidential election?

Strong midterm performance signals which party has momentum heading into 2028. The party that wins the House in 2026 often enters 2028 with wind at its back — and the candidates who perform well in 2026 governor and Senate races become natural presidential contenders.
