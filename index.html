About the term

Under the 20th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, a presidential term ends at noon on January 20th, four years after inauguration. Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 47th President of the United States on January 20, 2025. His term is set to expire on January 20, 2029, at 12:00:00 EST — the exact moment this clock counts down to.

The 20th Amendment

Ratified in 1933, the 20th Amendment moved the presidential inauguration from March 4 to January 20, reducing the "lame duck" period. It also establishes the exact noon deadline for the transfer of presidential power.

Can Trump run again?

The 22nd Amendment limits presidents to two terms. Trump served one term (2017–2021) and is currently in his second. This means he is constitutionally barred from seeking a third term in 2028.

Electoral College

The U.S. President is not elected by a direct popular vote. Instead, 538 electors — distributed by state population — cast the deciding votes. A candidate needs 270 electoral votes to win the presidency.

Inauguration Day

Inauguration Day is held on January 20 every four years. The incoming president takes the oath of office at the U.S. Capitol. The ceremony marks the peaceful transfer of power — a cornerstone of American democracy.

FAQ

When exactly does Trump's term end?

Donald Trump's presidential term ends on January 20, 2029, at 12:00:00 noon Eastern Standard Time (EST), which is 17:00:00 UTC. This is the moment the next president is sworn in and Trump's authority as president ceases.

Is this clock accurate?

Yes. The countdown uses your device's local clock synchronized to UTC, counting down to the fixed target of January 20, 2029, 17:00:00 UTC. The accuracy depends on your device's system clock.

How long is a U.S. presidential term?

A U.S. presidential term is exactly 4 years. Presidents can serve a maximum of two terms (8 years total), as established by the 22nd Amendment ratified in 1951.

Who will be the next U.S. president after Trump?

The next U.S. presidential election is scheduled for November 4, 2028. The winner will be inaugurated on January 20, 2029. Candidates and campaigns will emerge throughout 2027–2028.
